Roast Chicken

A Roast Chicken for a family dinner can be an ideal choice. A classic Chicken Roast recipe involves a basic blend of salt, pepper, garlic, onion and herbs. However, when it comes to Indian households, we love a generous spread of the spicy and aromatic masala marinade for the meat preparations. So why not make a roast chicken desi style? Here we share a roast chicken recipe that will not only give you juicy and moist chicken bites but will also satiate your taste buds with the splash of traditional Indian masalas.

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 Whole Chicken skin-on
  • 2½ tbsp Kashmiri Red Chilli powder
  • 1½ tbsp Ginger paste
  • 1 tbsp Garlic paste
  • ½ tbsp Pepper powder
  • 1 tbsp Cumin powder
  • 2 tbsp Honey
  • 1½ tbsp Vinegar
  • 50 g melted butter
  • Salt to taste
  • 3– 4 Potatoes quartered
  • 3 Onions quartered
  • 3 Pods Garlic whole halved
  • 1 Lemon sliced

INSTRUCTIONS OR STEPS

Pat the chicken dry. Then take a bowl and mix the ingredients such as chilli powder, ginger-garlic paste, pepper, cumin powder, honey, vinegar, butter and salt. Make a smooth paste. Lather this paste over the chicken. Make sure you spread the paste in the crevices as well. For an extra burst of flavour, use your fingers and loosen the skin away from the chicken breast and thighs and spread the marinade in these pockets over the meat. This way, you can trap the flavour between the meat and the skin. Cover the chicken and let it marinate for 2 hours.

Take the baking dish you will be using to roast the chicken. Add the vegetables such as onion, potatoes, garlic and lemon slices to the dish.

For the chicken to roast equally and retain the juices, tie the chicken legs together. Take a kitchen string and use it to tie the legs.

Transfer the chicken to the baking dish and bake it in the oven at 200 degrees celsius for 90 minutes or an hour. Make sure to keep brushing the chicken with butter or marinade every 30 minutes.

Once the chicken is evenly roasted, take the tray out and cover the whole chicken with a foil. Let it rest for 10 minutes. Then squeeze a bit of lemon on top of the chicken and serve it with the baked vegetables.

mutten

Mutton Curry or Lamb Curry - One of the finest Indian mutton recipes you should not miss to try it out. It's fuss free and uniquely delicious! Tender succulent goat meat or lamb meat cooked in onion tomato gravy with yogurt and basic spices. On your table in straight 30 minutes, this simple mutton curry is what you need to make yourself feel warm and content!

INGREDIENTS

  • 500 gm mutton
  • 2 tablespoon coriander powder
  • 4 onion
  • 2 teaspoon garlic paste
  • 4 clove
  • 5 green cardamom
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • salt as required
  • 2 cup water
  • 4 tablespoon ghee
  • 1/2 cup yoghurt (curd)
  • 2 teaspoon ginger paste
  • 2 teaspoon red chilli powder
  • 3 tomato
  • 8 peppercorns
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric

INSTRUCTIONS OR STEPS

Here is an excellent mutton recipe that you can easily make at home. To prepare this easy recipe, firstly, clean and wash the mutton pieces. Next, put a large pan over medium flame and melt ghee in it. Once the ghee has melted, add cardamom, cloves, black peppercorns, cinnamon stick. Saute these for a minute and add the sliced onions along with salt in it. Saute till the onion turns light brown in colour. Next, add the ginger paste along with garlic paste and cook the mixture until the raw smell disappears.

Now, add turmeric powder, red chilli powder and coriander powder to the fried onion and mix it well. Add the mashed tomatoes and mix them well with the other ingredients. Make sure the tomatoes turn pulpy and cook this mix until oil separates from the masala in the pan.

Now, add mutton pieces to this masala and mix it well. Ensure that mutton pieces are evenly coated with the masala. Pour the yoghurt in the mutton masala and mix it well. Let it cook for 4-5 minutes and, then, add water to it. Stir it well and check the seasoning once. Then, add chopped coriander leaves along with garam masala powder. Give it a quick stir. Cook it for another 25 minutes to ensure that mutton is no longer raw and is perfectly juicy.

Once done, take it off the flame and transfer the dish to a bowl. Serve it hot with rice or naan to enjoy! You can also garnish the dish with chopped green chillies and julienned ginger if you want. Traditional Rasmalai Recipe

Rasmalai or 'Rosomalai' (in bengali )is a very popular Bengali sweet, now it is also popular not only in India but also in Pakistan and Bangladesh. In Rasmalai, flat shaped spongy rasgullas or dumpling made with paneer or ricotta cheese is then soaked in sweet, thickened, chilled and saffron flavored milk. Traditional Rasmalai Recipe is an amazing recipe to indulge in this festive season to oomph the fervour of festive a notch higher!

Suji Halwa Recipe

Suji Halwa or Sooji Halwa is a popular Indian dessert which is especially prepared during various festivals and religious functions. Suji Halwa recipe is a quick and easy recipe to make this tasty Suji Halwa anytime at home. It hardly takes 15 minutes to cook which makes it quite handy at times. Semolina which is the key ingredient in Suji Halwa recipe is a good source of Protein and dietary fiber. Suji Halwa is often called Rava Sheera or simply Sheera in many places.
