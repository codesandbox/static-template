Operators in C++




    Operators are the symbols that perform operations on operands.

    Types of operators:


  • Airthematic operators: Operators that perform airthmatic operations.
    There are three types of airthmatic operations:

    • Unary operators: operators which require to work upon.There are two types of unary operators

      1. Postfix: Here firstly the value of a variable is used then changed(eg.- a++).
      2. Prefix: Here firstly the value of the variable is changed then used(eg.- ++a).

    • Binary operators: Here the operators require two operands to work on.
      Eg.: +,_.*,/,>, etc
      • a + b
      • a - b
      • a * b
      • a / b
      • a % b

    • Ternary Operators: Here the operators require three operands to work upon.
      Eg.: ?,:

  • Assignment Operators: These are used to assign values to the operands.
    Eg.:+=,-=, etc
    • a += b

  • Bitwise Operators: These operators works on bits.These are of three types:
    1. BITWISE And(&)
    2. BITWISE Or(|)
    3. BITWISE XOR(^)

  • Shift Operator: These are used to shift he values of the operands.
    There are three types of shift operators:
    • Left Shift
    • Right Shift
    • Unsigned Shift
